Melisa Cano is a pianist and sound artist focused on contemporary music, free impro, and new forms of sound expressions. Although she is an artist with a classical academic background, her artistic work aims to explore and experiment with sound using it as a bruit material: electronic textures created with SuperCollider/PureData, soundscape recordings, and extended techniques on various instruments... combining these materials with improvisation, intuition and our natural environment.

As a performer, she likes to explore different sound effects produced inside piano‚Äôs soundbox and strings, such as harmonics, scraping, or muting the strings, or with other materials like contact-microphones. She also programs her own digital instruments, including a tablet/iPad version of Oramics, the synthesizer originally designed by Daphne Oram in the 1960s.

She is co-artist of n0n0is, a free-impro duo where she plays the piano, oramics_live, programmed sensors, lyre and tambourine. They have participated in interdisciplinary projects and festivals. n0n0is released their first album in 2025: https://nonois.bandcamp.com

Melisa Cano holds BA in Piano Performance (Consmupa, Spain), a BA in Piano Pedagogy (Metropolia-University, Helsinki, Finland), a MA in Contemporary Music (Royal Conservatory, Madrid, Spain) and a MA in Electroacoustic Composition (Katarina Gurska Music Institute, Madrid, Spain).

ONiCrA is her artistic name for sound art works. Currently, her main projects are Oramics_back_to_life and SHIOPI.

Oramics_live is a live version of Oramics, the graphic sound synthesizer by Daphne Oram, for iPad/ tabled that she has programmed with PureData and Mobmuplat It was a final project for her studies in electroacoustic composition, and she uses as an instrument for free improvisation. Oramics_Live was presented at the Piksel 2024 Norway Festival in an online conference

SHIOPI is a sound installation/ sound object that explores silence trough three main concepts: nature, listening, sonification of the invisible sourround. It was programmed with supercollider and puredata, using different sensors for temperature, humidity and uv. She is also currently working in soundscape works processed in supercollider, for her first solo album, using field recordings of different places in Europe. SHIOPI was exhibited at the TTTarts 2025 festival in Ljubljana
